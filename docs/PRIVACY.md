# Privacy Policy — OpenCircuit

_Last updated: 2026-06-19_

OpenCircuit is an independent, **local-first** app that reads health metrics from a
compatible RingConn Gen 2 smart ring over Bluetooth and writes them into Apple
Health on your device. This policy explains what the app does and does not do with
your data.

## The short version

- **OpenCircuit has no servers and no account.** Nothing you record is uploaded
  anywhere by this app.
- Your health data stays **on your device** — in the app's local storage and, if you
  authorize it, in **Apple Health**.
- We do **not** collect, sell, share, or use your data for advertising or analytics.

## What data the app handles

- **Health & fitness metrics** read from your ring: heart rate, heart-rate
  variability, blood oxygen (SpO₂), skin temperature, respiratory rate, sleep
  stages, steps, and related activity values.
- **Profile inputs you enter** (age, height, weight, biological sex, goals, optional
  cycle logs) used only for on-device calculations.
- **Bluetooth** is used to connect to your ring. **Location** is used **only during
  an active workout** to map outdoor routes and to keep heart-rate recording alive
  while the screen is locked; it is never used at any other time and routes are not
  uploaded.

## Where the data goes

- Stored locally on your device in the app's database.
- Written to **Apple Health** only after you explicitly grant permission. Data in
  Apple Health is governed by Apple's privacy protections and your Health settings.
- Optionally exported by **you** (CSV/JSON) via the in-app export feature. Once you
  share an exported file, it is outside the app's control.

## What we do **not** do

- No analytics, tracking, advertising, or third-party SDKs.
- No transmission of your health data to OpenCircuit or any third party.
- No cloud account or sign-in.

## Your control

- Revoke Apple Health access anytime in **Settings ▸ Health ▸ Data Access &
  Devices**.
- Delete the app to remove its local data. Data already written to Apple Health is
  managed in the Health app.

## Not a medical device

OpenCircuit is **not a medical device** and does not provide medical advice or
diagnosis. Readings are derived from consumer ring sensors and may be inaccurate.
Consult a qualified professional for health concerns.
